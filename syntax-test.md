# Correct Markdown Syntax Guide

## Superscript (use ^ not *)
- ❌ Wrong: E = mc*2* (this makes italic)
- ✅ Correct: E = mc^2^

- ❌ Wrong: 1*st*, 2*nd*, 3*rd* (this makes italic)
- ✅ Correct: 1^st^, 2^nd^, 3^rd^

## Subscript (use ~ not *)
- ❌ Wrong: H*2*O (this makes italic)
- ✅ Correct: H~2~O

- ❌ Wrong: CO*2*
- ✅ Correct: CO~2~

- ❌ Wrong: C*6*H*12*O*6*
- ✅ Correct: C~6~H~12~O~6~

## Here's how they render:

### Superscript Examples
- Mathematical notation: E = mc^2^
- Footnote references: See note^[1]^
- Ordinal numbers: 1^st^, 2^nd^, 3^rd^

### Subscript Examples
- Chemical formulas: H~2~O, CO~2~, C~6~H~12~O~6~
- Mathematical indices: x~1~, x~2~, x~n~

## Quick Reference
| Syntax | Result | Use Case |
|--------|--------|----------|
| `^text^` | Superscript | Exponents, ordinals |
| `~text~` | Subscript | Chemical formulas, indices |
| `*text*` | *Italic* | Emphasis |
| `**text**` | **Bold** | Strong emphasis |
